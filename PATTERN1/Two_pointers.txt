1. Pattern Name & Category
**Name:** Two Pointers — Opposite Ends
**Category:** Array / String Traversal
**Also called:** Left-Right Pointer, Pinching Pointer

### 3. REAL-WORLD ANALOGY 

> Imagine you're searching for two people in a **height-sorted queue** (shortest to tallest) whose combined height equals exactly 10 feet.
>
> You put one hand at the **shortest person** (left) and one at the **tallest** (right).
> - Combined too tall? Move right hand left (shrink the tall side)
> - Combined too short? Move left hand right (grow the short side)
> - Exact match? Found them!
>
> You never need to check every pair — the **sorted order gives you direction**.

### 4. VISUAL DIAGRAM 
```
Array: [1, 2, 3, 4, 6]   Target = 9
        L              R

Step 1:  L=0, R=4  →  1+6=7  → too small → L++
        [1, 2, 3, 4, 6]
             L         R

Step 2:  L=1, R=4  →  2+6=8  → too small → L++
        [1, 2, 3, 4, 6]
                L      R

Step 3:  L=2, R=4  →  3+6=9  → FOUND! 
        [1, 2, 3, 4, 6]
                L      R

Key insight: When sum < target, we need bigger → move L right
             When sum > target, we need smaller → move R left
             Sorted order guarantees this is safe to do
