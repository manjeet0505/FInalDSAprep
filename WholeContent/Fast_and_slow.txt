PATTERN 3 — FAST & SLOW POINTERS 
Also called Floyd's Tortoise and Hare Algorithm

The Core Idea
Two pointers move in the same direction but at different speeds
Slow → moves 1 step at a time
Fast → moves 2 steps at a time

[1] → [2] → [3] → [4] → [5]

After step 1:
[1] → [2] → [3] → [4] → [5]

Why Is This Useful?
If list is LINEAR → fast reaches end first
If list has a CYCLE → fast laps slow → they MEET

TRIGGER PHRASES 
If problem says...Think..."find middle of linked list"Fast & Slow"detect cycle in linked list"Fast & Slow"find cycle start"Fast & Slow"find kth node from end"Fast & Slow"check if linked list is palindrome"Fast & Slow

Real World Analogy 
Two runners on a circular track
One runs twice as fast as the other
If track is circular → fast runner laps slow runner
They WILL meet eventually

If track is straight → fast runner just finishes first
